Design Language

Flexoki Ink, after dark

The same warm-grey ramp at night — brown-black surfaces, IBM Plex letterforms, cyan ink. For readers who keep the monitor on past midnight.

Status: candidate Audience: engineer-internal Dark only

01

Personality

Flexoki Ink is the dark inversion of the Flexoki philosophy. The neutral ramp never goes fully cold — even --paper at #100F0F is brown-black, not blue-black. The surfaces stack upward in luminance from the body background: code blocks and contracts sit on surface2, which is lighter than the page itself. This is the warm ink-paper relationship inverted: paper is dark, ink is the light text floating on it.

If Paper is ink soaking into a page, Ink is the page glowing under the lamp — the same warmth, lit from inside.

Cyan leads — links, the italic-em in the hero title, the active TOC entry, primary diagram arrows. Magenta supports — the eyebrow dot, marginalia counter, pill borders. Yellow is the warn color, replacing the orange of the light variant because orange on dark loses contrast against the warm surfaces. Body and headings are IBM Plex Sans because it carries enough engineering credibility for dark surfaces without going industrial; code is IBM Plex Mono so the two are visibly siblings.

Rules are 2px here — not 1px. Thin lines disappear on dark surfaces; the language compensates by making borders, hero footers, and details separators slightly more present.

02

When to pick this

Pick this when

The report is read on a dark editor or terminal-adjacent context. The audience explicitly prefers dark themes. The content is debugging-heavy, trace-heavy, or built around code blocks and sequence diagrams (where the dark surface lets the cyan arrows pop).

Don't pick this when

The report will be printed, exported to PDF for distribution, or read in a brightly-lit conference room — dark themes contrast-flip badly in those contexts. Use flexoki-paper. Also avoid for documents that lean heavily on illustrations or screenshots with light backgrounds.

Font stack variables

/* exact values — verbatim */
--serif: "IBM Plex Sans", "Inter", -apple-system, sans-serif;
--sans:  "IBM Plex Sans", "Inter", -apple-system, sans-serif;
--mono:  "IBM Plex Mono", "JetBrains Mono", "SF Mono", Menlo, monospace;

Flexoki Ink is a sans-led language; both --serif and --sans resolve to IBM Plex Sans. The "serif" alias exists only so canonical components written for the editorial register still resolve. Hierarchy comes from weight (300 / 400 / 500 / 600) and the Plex italic, not from a typeface switch.

Voice rules

  • Italic Plex Light for ledes, pull quotes, and the italic-em phrase inside h1: <h1>Title, <em>italic phrase</em></h1>.
  • Mono caps with letter-spacing for eyebrows, meta-row labels, marginalia, and SVG step numbers.
  • Never bold body prose for emphasis — use italic light instead.
  • The eyebrow dot is always var(--magenta).
  • Links are var(--cyan); hover collapses to var(--ink).
  • Rules and dividers are 2px minimum — thin lines disappear on dark surfaces.

Role assignment

  • Lead accent — --cyan. Links, italic-em in titles, active TOC, primary diagram arrows and hot nodes, progress bar fill.
  • Secondary — --magenta. Eyebrow dot, marginalia counter, pill borders, active-TOC counter prefix.
  • Warn — --yellow. Failure paths, deprecation, retry callouts. Yellow on warm-dark holds its weight better than orange.
  • The remaining hues (--green, --blue, --orange, --purple, --red) are syntax tokens and tertiary diagram roles.

Warning: Don't substitute --blue as the lead accent — blue on dark is a generic dashboard look. Flexoki Ink's identity is specifically the cyan/magenta duo against warm brown-black. Swap that and you've drifted into a different language.
05

Anti-patterns

  • No system-font defaults. IBM Plex must load.
  • No cool-grey or blue-black neutrals (#1A1B1F, #0F1115). The warm ramp is the brand.
  • No light themes — use flexoki-paper.
  • No bold body prose for emphasis. Italic Plex Light only.
  • No 1px rules on dark surfaces. Minimum 2px for borders, hero footers, details separators.
  • No saturated accents outside the Flexoki dark palette. No neon pink, no electric purple.
  • No heavy drop shadows. Cards lean on the surface-luminance stack, not on shadow.
  • No emoji in headings, callouts, or marginalia.
  • No paper grain or texture overlays — dark surfaces stay clean.

07

Diagram philosophy

Diagrams are ink on dark paper. Box outlines run through an SVG feTurbulence + feDisplacementMap pair (the #ink-bleed filter) so edges wobble like a fountain pen on absorbent stock, and strokes carry an irregular dash (9 2 4 3) that suggests ink pickup. Arrows are 2.5px cyan with a chiseled nib marker; each important arrow starts with a small terracotta (or magenta, for async) dot — the moment the pen touches the page. Body labels stay IBM Plex Sans; the small marginal "scrawl" is Plex italic light, slightly offset from the lines.

Alert region pattern

Arrow color

Default arrows: var(--cyan) at 2.5px with a chiseled nib marker (a path, not a triangle — it reads as a fountain-pen tip). Return paths use arrow-muted (var(--rule-strong)) with an irregular dash. Async paths use stroke-dasharray: 6 3. Warn paths use var(--yellow). Important origins carry a 3px terracotta (or magenta, for async) circle — the pen touch-down.
