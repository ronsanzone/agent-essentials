Design Language

Brutalist Print, set in a typewriter that never had a display face

A typewriter manuscript conceit: monospace headings, serif body, generous line-height, deep ink-blue as the only accent. The medium is paper that has been through a carriage.

Status: shipped Audience: long-form v1

01

BRUTALIST_PRINT.md

The conceit of Brutalist Print is that the document was typed on a machine which had only one face. Headings, code blocks, and any structural mark are therefore set in Courier Prime — the typewriter. Body prose is set in Spectral, a humanist serif designed for screen reading that stands in for Charter (Charter is not on Google Fonts; Spectral is the working substitute). The two faces sit next to each other on the page: the running text is a book; the structure is a manuscript.

A typewriter never had a separate display face. This styleguide preserves that fact and turns it into a design decision.

The line-height is intentionally generous (1.75). Letter-spacing on caps is tight. Headings are flush-left; nothing is centered except diagram captions. Em-dashes do real structural work — see the divider rule below — because the typewriter had em-dashes and not horizontal rules. A document in this language should feel like it has been typed, not laid out.

One accent only: ink-blue (#0000AA), the deeper, slightly desaturated blue of fountain-pen ink on bond paper. The accent does the work of italic emphasis, links, marginalia counters, and pull quotes. There is no warn color, no secondary color, no success color — manuscripts had ink and absence of ink, and that is the palette.

02

WHEN_TO_PICK.md

Pick this when

Essays. Design treatises. Long-form architectural prose where the reader will spend twenty or more minutes. Documents that aspire to be re-read on paper. Anything where the typewriter conceit reinforces the seriousness of the argument.

Don't pick this when

Operator runbooks (the conceit gets in the way). Short status reports (overdressed). Anything performance-dense — the generous line-height eats screen real estate. Audiences who read primarily on mobile.

Voice rules

  • Headings render in Courier Prime, often with a .md-style filename suffix as a self-aware nod (free: drop the suffix on documents that don't want the conceit foregrounded).
  • Italic-emphasis is set in Spectral italic, ink-blue. The italic does triple duty: stress, voice, and color accent.
  • Pull quotes are large Spectral italic, ink-blue, indented behind a thin black rule.
  • Em-dash dividers (.em-divider) separate sub-passages within a section — see §01 above for a live example. Used sparingly; one or two per long section.
  • Bold is allowed but rare; italic carries most emphasis.

Warning: Do not introduce red, green, or any second hue. The single-accent constraint is load-bearing — it is what distinguishes Brutalist Print from the other two variations. If a report needs three colors, it is the wrong language.
05

ANTI_PATTERNS.md

  • No serif headings. Headings are Courier Prime. Always. The typewriter conceit requires it.
  • No border-radius. A typewriter could not type a rounded corner.
  • No box-shadow. Paper sits flat.
  • No gradients. Ink does not gradient.
  • No second accent color. Ink-blue is the only non-neutral.
  • No dark mode. This language is paper. Paper is light.
  • No tight line-height. Body line-height is 1.7 or greater. The rhythm IS the language.
  • No centered headings or body text. Flush-left, like a manuscript draft. Diagram captions are the only exception (Spectral italic, centered).
  • No emoji.

07

DIAGRAM_PHILOSOPHY.md

Diagrams are typewriter line art. Boxes are drawn with +, -, and | characters set in Courier Prime; arrows are em-dashes (──>) rendered as SVG <text> rather than paths. The single ink-blue accent (#0000AA) appears only on the hot role; the alert region is signalled by stroke weight (3px black) — there is no red in this language. Captions are Spectral italic with a FIG. N. mono prefix; figures are separated by an em-dash divider rule.

The diagram is text, drawn at typewriter cadence. Boxes are + - | characters in Courier Prime; arrows are em-dashes; the only serif is in inline message labels and the caption.

Alert region pattern

The alert region is drawn as a 3px black outer frame around a 1px dashed inner frame — the doubled stroke is the alarm. There is no color shift anywhere in the region. An inner alert pill is built from + - | characters and carries a Courier Prime caps label ([#] RETRY POLICY ENGAGED); the [#] marker is the typewriter equivalent of a sidebar dingbat. Visible in FIG. 02. between steps 03 and 04.

LockedOuter frame: 3px black solid. Inner frame: 1px black dashed. Alert pill: drawn from + - | glyphs in Courier Prime caps. Sidebar marker: [!] or [#] in caps.
FreeRegion size, exact alert copy, choice of dingbat marker ([!], [#], [*]).

Arrow rendering

Arrows are <text> elements containing em-dash sequences, not SVG <path>. Default: solid black em-dashes ──>. Muted return: grey em-dashes with spaces <─ ─ ─. Async: dashed em-dashes ─ ─ ─>. Warn: double-rule glyphs ═══> for the failure-path arrow.

Caption convention

Each caption opens with a Courier Prime caps prefix FIG. NN. followed by a Spectral italic sentence describing what the figure shows. Figures are separated by an em-dash divider rule (— — — — —) running the full text-column width.
