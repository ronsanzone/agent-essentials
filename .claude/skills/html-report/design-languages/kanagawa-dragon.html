Design Language

Kanagawa Dragon, warm storm

A warmer dark variant of Kanagawa — brown-black backgrounds, surimi-orange as the lead accent, sakura-pink as the characterful secondary. Built for long-form prose that wants heat rather than the Wave's cool detachment.

Variant: dragon Mode: dark · warm Body: Crimson Pro
LockedCrimson Pro h1 at weight 500 with surimi-orange italic <em>, italic 400 lede, mono caps eyebrow with sakura-pink glow dot, low-opacity surimi-orange radial glow in lower-right of hero on a bg-darkest base.
FreePadding, lede max-width, hero glow position, footer as .meta-row or .meta pills.
01

Personality

Dragon is what happens when you tip Kanagawa toward warmth. The background is no longer the cool sumi-ink indigo of Wave — it is a warmer brown-black, the colour of lacquered wood. The lead accent steps from crystal-blue to surimi-orange, and a characterful sakura-pink takes the secondary slot, used very deliberately — eyebrow dot, marginalia numbers, pull-quote rule. The effect is a register that feels like reading by firelight rather than moonlight.

Dragon is for prose that has opinions. Wave whispers; Dragon insists.

Crimson Pro is the load-bearing font choice. It is an old-style serif with a strong text feel — generous x-height, robust italics, weights that hold up well at body sizes. It is the right voice for documents that will argue with the reader: design decisions, postmortems where opinions matter, ramp-up tours where the author is explicitly guiding interpretation. Pair it with JetBrains Mono in the usual technical slots.

02

When to pick this

Pick this when

Prose-led documents with strong narrative voice — design decision write-ups, opinionated architecture proposals, postmortems where interpretation matters. Long-form essays on engineering systems that benefit from a warm reading register at night.

Don't pick this when

Cool, neutral reference docs — Wave fits better. Light-mode reading — reach for Lotus. Heavy diagram-density tours where the warm palette competes with the diagram-kit's role colors — Wave's cool palette gives the diagram-kit more headroom.

Voice rules

  • Italic Crimson Pro for ledes and pull quotes — the italic is essential, Crimson italic is one of the strongest italics in any free serif.
  • Mono caps for eyebrows and marginalia. Sakura-pink for the marginalia number — this is one of Dragon's most distinctive touches.
  • Pull quotes are large (27px) and set in surimi-orange italic with a 2px sakura-pink left rule. They are the loudest gesture this language permits.
  • Never bold body prose for emphasis. Reserve bold weights for h4 and the <strong> label inside callouts.
  • Eyebrow dot is sakura-pink with a soft glow shadow — the smallest pink touch, but unmistakable.

05

Anti-patterns

  • No cool blue or teal as primary accent — that would collapse Dragon back into Wave. Crystal-blue exists in the palette only as the code-block type token, never on UI chrome.
  • No Inter, Roboto, Helvetica, or any sans for body. The Crimson Pro register defines this variant.
  • No light backgrounds — that role belongs to Lotus.
  • No three-accent UI. Surimi-orange and sakura-pink only. Adding a third hue (carp-yellow on chrome, dragon-red on borders) dilutes the duo.
  • No drop-shadows heavier than 0 14px 36px rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.30).
  • No emoji or kanji as decoration. The voice is in the typography.
  • No pure white #FFFFFF for text. Fuji-white is the brand and stark white reads as a different variant.

07

Diagram philosophy

Dragon's diagrams are angular and dramatic — sumi-ink-slash arrows (heavier 3px brushstrokes), octagonal nodes carved from <polygon>, dragon-scale pattern fills on external, sakura-pink calligraphic dots near labels. Captions stay in Crimson Pro italic, prefixed with surimi-orange 図 X. ("zu" — "figure"). Every connector curves; straight lines are forbidden.

Alert region pattern

Failure regions are framed by three stacked autumn-red strokes at descending opacity (1.0 / 0.55 / 0.35) and descending dash density — the multi-stroke layering reads as a rough, brushed border, not a clean print. An inline alert pill (16% autumn-red fill + solid 1.4px border + mono caps label + autumn-red ink dot) sits inside the region. Visible above between messages 03 and 04.

Arrow color

Default sumi-slash arrows: surimi-orange at 2px, round caps, quadratic curve. Returns / mutes: bg-dim at 1.2px. Async: dashed surimi-orange at 2px (stroke-dasharray: 6 4). Warn: autumn-red at 2.2px.
