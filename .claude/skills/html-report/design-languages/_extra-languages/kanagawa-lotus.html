Design Language

Kanagawa Lotus, quiet paper

A meditative light variant of Kanagawa — aged-paper cream against slate-lavender ink, with deep wave-teal as the only accent. Built for the document that wants to disappear into reading.

Variant: lotus Mode: light Body: Cormorant Garamond
LockedCormorant Garamond h1 at weight 400 with wave-blue-2 italic <em>, italic 400 lede in slate-lavender, mono caps eyebrow with wave-blue-2 dot (no glow), no hero gradient — the paper itself is the texture.
FreePadding (generous is the brief), lede max-width, hero footer style.
01

Personality

Lotus is the quietest Kanagawa variant. Where Wave whispers in indigo and Dragon insists in orange, Lotus says almost nothing and leaves the page space to breathe. The background is a warm cream — aged Japanese paper, not bright printer-white. The ink is slate-lavender (#545464), never pure black: this is the colour discipline that keeps Lotus inside the Kanagawa family even when every other token has flipped to light.

A page in Lotus should feel like reading on washi by daylight — present, but not loud.

Cormorant Garamond is the typographic engine here. It is a delicate revival serif with high stroke contrast and elegant italics; it pairs naturally with the meditative paper and pushes back against any temptation to fill the space with chrome. The body font is set smaller than the dark variants (15px vs 16–17px) and the line-height is more generous (1.8) — the result is a column that wants to be read slowly.

One accent. That is the discipline. --wave-blue-2 is the deep teal of the wave's underbelly — links, italic em in titles, the eyebrow dot, the marginalia counter, callout border, pull quote. There is no secondary accent. Warn exists, but it lives in the palette so quietly that you forget it is there until it appears.

02

When to pick this

Pick this when

The document is read in daylight, on paper-like surfaces — print to PDF, share to e-ink, embed in a static documentation site. Long-form essays where the goal is reading flow, not visual variety. Reference material that will be returned to many times — the muted palette will not fatigue.

Don't pick this when

The document has many architectural diagrams that need distinct role colors — Lotus's single-accent discipline collapses the role taxonomy. Reports with heavy warn / failure-path content — surface autumn-red breaks the meditative feel. Anything read at night.

Voice rules

  • Generous whitespace is part of the brand. Section margin is 128px (vs 96px in Wave/Dragon); hero padding is 144px top.
  • One accent only — --wave-blue-2. Do not introduce sakura-pink, surimi-orange, or carp-yellow as UI accents.
  • No shadows on cards. Lotus is flat — depth is signaled by border alone.
  • Pull quotes are italic Cormorant in wave-blue-2 with a thin rule-base left border. Same accent as everything else.
  • Mono caps with 0.22em letter-spacing for eyebrows — wider than the dark variants, reinforcing the slow reading rhythm.
  • The eyebrow dot is wave-blue-2 with no glow — the dark variants use a glow shadow; Lotus does not.

Diagram-kit aliases

Because Lotus has no true secondary, the diagram-kit aliases collapse --secondary to a desaturated ink-mid — so .external nodes in diagrams read as "different but not loud."

--accent:         var(--wave-blue-2);
--accent-soft:    rgba(45, 79, 103, 0.10);
--secondary:      var(--ink-mid);
--secondary-soft: rgba(109, 109, 133, 0.08);
Warning: Do not introduce surimi-orange or sakura-pink for UI accents in Lotus. The single-accent discipline is the brand. If a document genuinely needs a second accent to communicate, the variant choice is wrong — use Wave or Dragon.
05

Anti-patterns

  • No pure white #FFFFFF background. The paper is #F2ECBC; stark white reads as a different brand entirely.
  • No pure black #000000 text. Ink is slate-lavender #545464 — this preserves Kanagawa's colour character in light mode.
  • No secondary accent. Wave-blue-2 alone.
  • No drop-shadows. Cards are flat; depth is communicated by border alone.
  • No sans-serif for body. Cormorant is the voice.
  • No tight section spacing. Lotus needs whitespace; do not collapse section { margin-bottom } below 96px to save vertical space.
  • No emoji in headings or eyebrows.
  • No saturated callout washes. The accent-soft is 10% opacity by design.

Every component a report uses, in Lotus. The stable CSS selectors carry across all design languages; only their paint changes.

1. Hero .hero

See the document's own hero above. No gradient. The paper is the texture; the slate-lavender ink and the wave-blue-2 italic em provide all the visual interest the language permits at the top of a document.

2. Section .section

Section bottom margin is 128px — visibly more whitespace than the dark variants. The wave-blue-2 mono .num in the left gutter is the single brand-accent touch at the section level.

LockedCormorant h2 at 34px / weight 500, wave-blue-2 mono number, position: absolute; left: -64px, section bottom margin ≥96px (default 128px).
FreeExact margin (96–144px), h2 weight (400–500).

3. Card .card

A self-contained card

Cards use lotus-card (#F7F2C6), one shade lighter than the paper. The border is rule-soft and there is no shadow. The radius is small (6px) — Lotus does not lean on rounded corners for personality.

Lockedlotus-card fill, 1px solid rule-soft border, no shadow, small radius (4–8px).
FreeExact radius, padding.

4. Callout .callout

Note: Default callouts use a 10%-opacity wave-blue-2 wash with a 2px solid wave-blue-2 left border. The thinner border (vs 4px in Wave/Dragon) and the lower-opacity wash keep the callout quiet.
Warning: Warn uses a muted red (#C84053) — chosen to read as alarm against the cream paper without becoming the loudest thing on the page. Reserve for failure paths only.
Locked2px solid left border + 10%-opacity wash. Warn swaps both colors only.
FreeWash opacity (8–12%), border-radius (0–6px), padding.

5. Pull quote .quote

A pull quote in Lotus is a single italic line in wave-blue-2 — the only place outside an <em> that the accent color appears in running text.
LockedCormorant italic, wave-blue-2 color, ≥22px, thin rule-base left border.
FreeExact font-size (22–26px), padding.

6. Details block details

Step 1. Initial step (open by default)

Cormorant summary at 21px / weight 500 in wave-blue-2. The mono + / − marker is ink-soft — Lotus does not use a second color for the marker the way Wave (wave-aqua) and Dragon (sakura-pink) do.

Step 2. Subsequent step

The rule-soft top-border between details items is one shade lighter than the rule used elsewhere — Lotus uses subtle rule weight differences instead of accent color to communicate hierarchy.

LockedCormorant summary in wave-blue-2 at ~21px, rule-soft top border, ink-soft mono marker.

07

Diagram philosophy

Lotus's diagrams use the cream-paper surface as the substrate. Default boxes have rule-soft borders and lotus-card fills — visibly distinct from the page but not loud. Hot nodes use the 10%-opacity wave-blue-2 wash with a wave-blue-2 border. External nodes use the desaturated ink-mid pairing rather than a second color — this is the visible consequence of the single-accent discipline.

Alert region pattern

Lotus's alert region uses a 6%-opacity warn wash with a dashed warn border, and the inline alert pill stacks a small warn dot, mono caps label, and italic Cormorant detail at 10%-opacity background. The pattern is less dramatic than Wave/Dragon by design — Lotus wants the alert noticed, not shouted.

LockedRegion: 6% warn wash + dashed warn border. Alert pill: 10% fill + solid warn border + mono caps label + optional dot.
FreeRegion size, alert copy, dot presence.

Arrow color

Default arrows: wave-blue-2 at 1.5px — thinner than the dark variants' 2px, in keeping with Lotus's lighter visual weight. Returns/mutes use rule-strong. Async: dashed wave-blue-2. Warn: solid warn-red.
