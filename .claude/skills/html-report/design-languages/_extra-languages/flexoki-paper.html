Design Language

Flexoki Paper, inked on warm stock

A prose-led register for engineers who write the way they read — at a desk, sequentially, with the lamp on and the notebook open.

Status: candidate Audience: engineer-internal Light only
LockedLiterata h1 with opsz 72, blue italic <em>, Literata italic lede at opsz 24, mono caps eyebrow with 6px red dot, surface1 → paper linear gradient.
FreePadding, lede max-width, hero footer as .meta-row key/values or .meta pill chips.
01

Personality

Flexoki Paper takes Steph Ango's "inky color scheme for prose and code" and renders it as a long-form reading surface. The warm-grey ramp never goes fully cold — even the rules and borders carry a paper tone. Body and headings are Literata; code, eyebrows, and marginalia are JetBrains Mono. The lead accent is blue — used on links, the italic-em phrase inside hero titles, the active TOC entry, and primary diagram nodes. Red is the secondary touch — the eyebrow dot, the marginalia counter, ticket pills. Orange is reserved for warnings.

A page in this language should feel like ink soaking into uncoated paper — warm, soft-edged, and quietly opinionated about what matters.

The paper background is load-bearing. #FFFCF0 is not "almost white" — it is specifically warmer than any cool grey, and the subtle radial-gradient grain reinforces that warmth without becoming texture. Drop the grain and the page goes flat; raise it and it becomes a stationery sample. The 17px body and 1.68 line-height give the page a slower cadence than a typical web doc; that cadence is the language.

02

When to pick this

Pick this when

The report is essay-shaped — a long-form ramp-up, a deep operator reference, an annotated tour of a system. The reader will sit with it for 15+ minutes. The content benefits from a serif voice and a generous reading rhythm.

Don't pick this when

The reader will skim a dashboard in 90 seconds — Flexoki Paper's rhythm is too slow. Pick a denser sans-led language. Also avoid when the document needs dark mode (use flexoki-ink) or when the brand is mono-first (use flexoki-prose).

Font stack variables

/* exact values — verbatim */
--serif: "Literata", "Iowan Old Style", "Charter", Georgia, serif;
--sans:  "Literata", "Iowan Old Style", "Charter", Georgia, serif;
--mono:  "JetBrains Mono", "SF Mono", "IBM Plex Mono", Menlo, monospace;

Yes — --serif and --sans resolve to the same family. Flexoki Paper is intentionally a single-typeface body experiment; the variation comes from Literata's optical-size axis, not from a serif/sans split.

Font role assignments

RoleFontNotes
Body prosevar(--serif) — Literata17px, line-height 1.68
Display / headingsvar(--serif) — LiterataUse opsz variation axis (24–72)
Code, eyebrows, marginalia, pills, meta keysvar(--mono) — JetBrains Mono

Optical-size settings

  • h1: font-variation-settings: "opsz" 72 — Literata's display end of the axis.
  • h2: font-variation-settings: "opsz" 60.
  • Ledes and pull quotes: font-variation-settings: "opsz" 24 — body-leaning, for italic phrases that should read as part of the prose register.

Voice rules

  • Italic serif for ledes, pull quotes, and italic-em phrases inside h1 titles: <h1>Title, <em>italic phrase</em></h1>.
  • Mono caps with letter-spacing for eyebrows and meta-row labels.
  • Never bold body prose for emphasis — use italic instead.
  • The eyebrow dot is always var(--red) — Flexoki Paper's smallest red touch.
  • Links are var(--blue); hover collapses to var(--ink). No purple visited state.

Role assignment

  • Lead accent — --blue. Links, italic-em in titles, active TOC, primary diagram arrows and hot nodes.
  • Secondary — --red. Eyebrow dot, marginalia counter, pill borders, active-TOC counter prefix.
  • Warn — --orange. Failure paths, deprecation, retry callouts.
  • The remaining hues (--green, --cyan, --yellow, --purple, --magenta) are reserved for syntax tokens in code blocks and the occasional categorical diagram. They are not load-bearing brand colors.

Diagram-kit aliases

--accent:         var(--blue);
--accent-soft:    #DDE7F2;
--secondary:      var(--red);
--secondary-soft: #F3D9D5;
Warning: Do not promote orange to a lead accent or treat green as a "success" brand color. Flexoki's strength is restraint — blue leads, red supports, everything else stays in code blocks or tertiary diagram roles.
05

Anti-patterns

  • No system-font defaults. Literata must load — fallback to Iowan/Charter/Georgia only if the network fails.
  • No cool-grey neutrals. The ramp is warm; #F5F5F5 kills the brand.
  • No dark themes — use flexoki-ink instead.
  • No bold body prose for emphasis. Italic only.
  • No saturated accents outside the Flexoki palette. No electric blue, no neon green, no pastel pinks.
  • No shadows heavier than 0 8px 24px rgba(28, 27, 26, 0.03). Heavy shadows destroy the paper feel.
  • No full-bleed photos or decorative gradients beyond the subtle paper grain.
  • No emoji in headings, callouts, or marginalia.
  • No purple visited-link state — links stay blue regardless of history.

07

Diagram philosophy

Diagrams here are quiet and precise — paper-fill rectangles, thin borders, blue arrows. SVG text uses Literata (matching body) so the diagram and the surrounding prose share a register. Use hot sparingly — only on the step the prose is currently discussing.

LockedSVG body text is Literata (matches body prose). Inline annotations are Literata italic in --faint. Step numbers and alert text use JetBrains Mono with letter-spacing — red for steps, orange for alerts.
FreeWhether to use step numbers at all (small diagrams omit them), exact letter-spacing (0.10–0.16em), annotation placement (above arrow vs. below).

Alert region pattern

When a diagram has a failure path or exceptional region, wrap the affected messages in an orange-tinted dashed rectangle and place an inline alert pill (warn-soft fill + orange border + orange-colored mono caps text) explaining the consequence. The pattern is visible in the sequence diagram above between messages 03 and 04.

LockedRegion background uses rgba(188, 82, 21, 0.06) + dashed orange border. The inline alert pill stacks a colored dot, a mono-caps label, and a Literata italic detail.
FreeRegion size, exact alert pill copy, whether to include the colored dot.

Arrow color

Default arrows: var(--blue) at 2px. Return paths use arrow-muted (var(--rule-strong)) so direction is legible at a glance. Async/fire-and-forget paths use stroke-dasharray: 6 3.

Caption

Italic Literata, var(--faint), 14px, centered under the diagram with margin-top: 12px.

Exemplar reports

Read at least one before writing a fresh report in this language. Exemplars are evidence of vocabulary deployed in real content — not templates to clone.

  • Pending — a long-form ramp-up tour rendered in Flexoki Paper at full scale. Reference for "everything this language can do."
  • Pending — a mid-length operator reference. Same vocabulary at smaller scale.

Two reports in this language should be visibly the same voice but visibly not xeroxed. If they feel identical, one of them is over-templated. If they feel like different brands, one of them broke the locked stack.

Lock the stack, free the composition — that is the only rule that matters.
