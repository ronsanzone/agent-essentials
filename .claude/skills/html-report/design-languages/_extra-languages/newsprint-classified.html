Design Language · RUSH

Newsprint Classified — set in column inches

A typographic register for high-density operator references, dense status reports, ledger-style summaries, and any document that wants the busy authority of the back-of-paper classified section. Tighter than broadsheet. Louder than aged. Built for readers who need many facts on one page and don't want to scroll.

01

Personality

Newsprint Classified is the CLASSIFIED ADS SECTION of the paper. The register is dense, condensed, busy, and load-bearing on red. Every section heading has a red flag. Every important pill is red. Body type is small (13.5px) and tight (line-height 1.45). Faces are condensed: Bebas Neue for display, Oswald for section labels and h3/h4, Roboto Condensed for body. The classified section gets more information onto one page than any other newspaper register — that's the goal here.

More facts. Less white space. Read it standing up.

The Bebas / Oswald / Roboto Condensed stack is load-bearing. Bebas Neue is the "FREE TO A GOOD HOME" headline face — narrow, all caps at large size, immediately readable as classified. Oswald carries the section-label work — caps with letter-spacing, weight 600–700. Roboto Condensed is the body workhorse: a condensed sans dense enough to fit two columns into a single screen width. Mono code is JetBrains Mono. Red is everywhere — bordered pills (red), table headers (red), section nums (red), eyebrow (red), callout strong (red).

02

When to pick this

Pick this when

Operator references with high data density. Status dashboards. Release notes with many bullet points. Ledger-style writeups where information count matters more than reading rhythm. Anything that would otherwise need two pages.

Don't pick this when

The reader will sit and absorb for 20 minutes — use newsprint-broadsheet or editorial-parchment. The content is reflective, historical, or storytelling — use newsprint-aged. The audience is non-technical and the density would overwhelm.

Especially well-suited for

API reference indexes. Feature flag tables. Multi-row config comparisons. SLO summaries. The "everything you need on one page" report.

Voice rules

  • Body line-height is 1.45 — not 1.6. Tightness is part of the brand.
  • Body font-size is 13.5px — small. The reader is meant to lean in.
  • EVERY h2 wears a red square bullet (■) and sits on a black ink chip. The chip is what makes a section read as a classified ad heading.
  • Pills are red-filled by default with a ★ star prefix (classified-ad-marker convention). .outline swaps to red border + transparent. .ink swaps to a black-filled variant.
  • Table headers are red. Always. White text on red is the classified table signature.
  • Lede uses CSS column-count: 2 — multi-column body where space allows is part of the register.
  • Callout strong labels are inverse chips (red-filled, white text) — they read as classified-ad badges.
  • Never add airy whitespace. The anti-pattern is the broadsheet airy-page feel.

05

Anti-patterns

  • NO whitespace luxuries. Margins are compact. Padding is small. Gaps are small.
  • No serif body. Roboto Condensed only.
  • No light weights on display type. Bebas Neue is 400, Oswald lives at 600–700.
  • No second saturated accent. Red, ink, and neutral cream — nothing else.
  • No rounded corners. Square edges everywhere (radius: 0). Even pills are square.
  • No dark themes. Classified is light-only.
  • No shadow effects. The visual depth comes from the dense rule-work.
  • No emoji. The square bullet (■), star (★), and triangle (▸) glyphs are the allowed iconography.
  • No line-height above 1.5. Density requires tightness.
  • No body font-size above 14.5px. The reader is supposed to fit a lot on a screen.

07

Diagram philosophy

Classified diagrams are compact and loud. Square corners, thin black borders, red on the hot path, Oswald caps labels inside boxes (not Georgia serif — that would be broadsheet). Diagrams are smaller than broadsheet equivalents — pack them in.

Alert region pattern

When a diagram has a failure path, wrap affected messages in a warn-tinted dashed rectangle and place an Oswald-caps alert banner. The classified variant pushes the warn-bg opacity to 0.10 (heavier than broadsheet's 0.08).

LockedWarn-tinted bg rgba(154, 99, 0, 0.10) + dashed warn border. Alert banner is Oswald caps in warn with optional warn dot.
FreeRegion size, exact alert copy.

Arrow color

Default arrows: --red-flag-nyt. 2px stroke for primary, 1.5px --rule-strong for muted/return. Async = dashed red.

Caption
