A Design Language, in the Old Manner

Newsprint Aged, discovered in the stacks

A typographic register for historical retrospectives, decade-in-review writeups, archival reports, and any document that wants to feel like it was set in lead a century ago and survived intact.

Status: draft Audience: archival v. i
LockedIM Fell English h1 with deep-red italic <em>, Libre Baskerville italic lede, sepia-ink throughout (never pure black), heavy paper-grain texture (opacity 0.07–0.10), paper-deeper → paper-aged gradient hero.
FreePadding, lede max-width, hero footer style, presence of double-rule under hero.
i.

Personality

Newsprint Aged is the "discovered in an old library" register. The document feels like a chapter opening from a printed volume that has been on the shelf since 1898 — warm aged paper, sepia ink that was once black but has mellowed, and a serif (IM Fell English) that is literally a digital revival of 17th-century Caslon. Heavy paper-grain texture is the distinguishing feature: where other languages hint at paper, Aged shows the wear.

An old report has earned the right to look its age — the typesetter is long retired; the ink has had time to settle.

The IM Fell / Libre Baskerville split is load-bearing. IM Fell English carries the historical character — irregular letterforms, the visible printer's hand. Libre Baskerville is the body workhorse: a transitional serif strong enough to anchor long retrospective prose. Mono is JetBrains Mono, but appears sparingly — old documents have little need for modern code voice. The accent is --red-dark #9B1C1C, a deeper red than the NYT flag — it reads as aged ink, not as a section marker.

ii.

When to pick this

Pick this when

Decade-in-review retrospectives. Historical writeups and archival reports. The 100-year anniversary essay. Documents that want gravity through age, not authority through modernity. Storytelling pieces where the visual register is part of the narrative.

Don't pick this when

The content is operational or technical-current — use newsprint-broadsheet or editorial-parchment. The audience expects modern presentation. Accessibility is a primary concern (the aged paper background reduces contrast versus pure white).

Voice rules

  • Ink is never pure black. Always --sepia-ink #4A3528 or --sepia-deep #2F2118. Pure black against aged paper looks anachronistic.
  • Section numbers are lowercase roman (i., ii., iii.) — not Arabic — set in IM Fell italic.
  • The accent red is --red-dark #9B1C1C (deeper, less garish than NYT red). Used for italic em, pill borders, callout accents, diagram hot path.
  • Captions, eyebrow text, h3, table headers, summary, h4 are commonly IM Fell italic. The italic is the brand voice.
  • Paper-grain texture (body::before + body::after) is visible at 0.07–0.10 opacity — heavier than any other newsprint variation. The aged paper is meant to be seen.
  • Never use heavy display weights. IM Fell only ships at 400. The historical character requires the original weight; bold simulates an effect IM Fell does not have.

v.

Anti-patterns

  • No pure black ink. Anything that should read as "dark" is sepia (#4A3528 or #2F2118).
  • No white backgrounds. Cards are --bg-card (aged paper, deeper); code is --bg-code (deeper still).
  • No bright saturated reds. The red is #9B1C1C — aged ink red.
  • No sans-serif anywhere except, optionally, system fallbacks. This is a fully serif language.
  • No dark themes. The aged paper register requires light paper.
  • No heavy display weights — IM Fell at 400 only. Faux-bold simulates type effects IM Fell never carried.
  • No modern flat geometry. Card corners are 2px max (radius 999px allowed on pills only).
  • No emoji. Period.
  • Paper-grain texture is mandatory. Removing body::before + body::after strips the language of its defining feature.

vii.

Diagram philosophy

Aged diagrams are sparingly used — old documents reach for prose first, illustration second. When they appear, they look like steel-engraved figures from a 19th-century volume: thin borders, aged-paper fills, sepia text, deep-red ink on the hot path.

Alert region pattern

When a diagram has a failure path, wrap affected messages in a warn-tinted dashed rectangle and place an IM Fell italic alert banner inside. Lowercase, like a footnote in an old volume.

LockedWarn-tinted background uses rgba(138, 85, 0, 0.10) + dashed warn border. Alert banner uses IM Fell italic in warn, lowercase.
FreeRegion size, exact copy, presence of dot.

Arrow color

Default arrows: --red-dark. 2px stroke for primary flow, 1.5px for secondary references via --rule-strong. Async dispatch is dashed red-dark.

Caption

IM Fell italic, --ink-faint, 14px, centered. Reads as a 19th-century plate caption.
