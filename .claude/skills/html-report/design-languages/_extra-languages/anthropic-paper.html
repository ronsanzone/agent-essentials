Design Language

Anthropic Paper, the corporate canonical

A faithful Anthropic brand register — Poppins display, Lora body, terracotta accent on cream — for technical announcements, public-facing research notes, and reports that speak in the company's own voice.

Status: candidate Audience: public + engineer v0.1
LockedPoppins display, Lora italic <em> in --orange, Lora italic lede, mono caps eyebrow with 6px --blue dot, bg-soft → bg linear gradient on cream paper.
FreePadding, lede max-width, hero footer as .meta-row key/values or .meta pill chips.
01

Personality

Anthropic Paper is the brand canonical — the register you would publish a public technical announcement in, or a research note that needs to feel like it came from the company rather than from any one engineer. Poppins gives the document its geometric, humanist confidence; Lora gives the body and italics a literary warmth that's deliberately not Silicon Valley. The terracotta — #d97757, not "AI blue" — is the differentiator: warmth where every other AI lab leans cool.

A report in this language should read like a thoughtful Anthropic blog post that happens to have engineering depth — not like a startup announcement.

Lead accent is --orange: links, italic-em in titles, active TOC, primary diagram nodes. Secondary is --blue, used sparingly — eyebrow dot, marginalia number, pill borders. The tertiary --green exists in the palette but appears only on the .callout.success variant; it is not a general-purpose accent. The point of the duo is restraint: warmth lead, cool support.

02

When to pick this

Pick this when

The report represents the company, not a single engineer's voice. Public research notes, technical announcements, capability documents, customer-facing safety memos. Anything that would feel right next to a corporate blog post.

Don't pick this when

The report is product-internal and would feel overdressed in corporate brand — pick anthropic-clay for the product-UI register. The report is academic in posture and benefits from restraint — pick anthropic-research. The report is long-form engineering ramp-up — editorial-parchment is still the right choice.

RoleFontNotes

Voice rules

  • Headings are Poppins. Italic phrases inside headings are Lora italic. The visible serif/sans seam is intentional — it is what makes the language feel like Anthropic and not like every Tailwind landing page.
  • Mono caps with letter-spacing for eyebrows, meta-row labels, marginalia counter.
  • Never use Lora for h1/h2/h3 — the display face must remain Poppins. The serif appears in body and in italic-em inside the display.
  • Pull quotes are Lora italic in --orange, set off by a thin left rule.
  • The eyebrow dot is always --blue — the language's smallest cool-color touch, deliberately reserved.

05

Anti-patterns

  • No Inter, Roboto, Arial, or system-font defaults — the brand requires Poppins + Lora.
  • No Lora in h1/h2/h3 — display must remain Poppins. Lora belongs in body and in italic-em inside the display.
  • No "AI blue" gradients, no purple-to-pink hero washes, no dark themes (this is a daylight brand).
  • No third primary accent. Lead orange + supporting blue is load-bearing.
  • No emoji in headings or callouts.
  • No shadow heavier than 0 8px 24px rgba(40, 30, 18, 0.035) — heavier shadows make the cream paper look like a product chrome surface.
  • No full-bleed photographs or hero imagery; this is a typographic brand register.
  • No use of --green as a general accent — it lives in .callout.success and nowhere else.

A self-contained card

07

Diagram philosophy

Diagrams in Anthropic Paper are calm and considered. Boxes have thin rules, generous padding, cream fills, and terracotta arrows. Text inside diagrams uses Poppins (matching the display face) so the diagram reads as part of the document, not as an imported screenshot.

Exemplar reports

Exemplars do not yet exist for this candidate language. Once anthropic-paper is promoted out of _candidates/, the first two production reports to use it should be linked here. Until then, treat this styleguide itself as the canonical reference.

  • Expected first use: a public-facing technical announcement or capability summary.
  • Expected second use: a longer-form research-adjacent note that is not pure academic preprint material (those go to anthropic-research).
Two reports in this language should feel like two pages of the same Anthropic publication — visibly the same voice, visibly not xeroxed.
