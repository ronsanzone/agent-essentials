Design Language

Anthropic Research, set as a preprint

A restrained, single-accent register patterned after an AI safety paper or academic preprint — Source Serif Pro throughout, generous whitespace, a single terracotta touch where signal is required.

Status: candidate Audience: research v0.1
LockedSource Serif Pro display + body (single face family), narrow 760px main column with 64px right padding, generous 96px section gutters, single --orange accent applied sparingly.
FreeHero padding, lede max-width, meta-row vs pill .meta layout.
01

Personality

Anthropic Research is the most restrained variation in the family. It is patterned on the visual conventions of an AI safety preprint or a journal article — a single serif face carrying everything from h1 to body to pull quote, a single accent color used so sparingly that each appearance carries weight, and a narrow text column that lets the prose breathe inside generous margins.

The register here is academic, not corporate. Where anthropic-paper would say "we announce," this language says "we observe." Where anthropic-clay would wear its terracotta confidently, this language uses the same hex with a kind of reluctance — a thin rule on a callout, a small dot in the eyebrow, the active link in the TOC. Nothing else.

Restraint is the message. If you can remove an accent and the document still reads correctly, remove it.

There is no secondary color. No --blue, no --green, no amber-for-warn. Warn becomes a deeper shade of the same orange (--orange-deep), used in italic context. The single-accent rule is what makes this language feel like an academic paper rather than a brand artifact.

02

When to pick this

Pick this when

The report would naturally be published as a PDF preprint. AI safety write-ups, alignment research notes, capability evaluations, mechanistic interpretability findings. Documents whose authorship feels collective and considered, where dense citation and figure-numbering matter more than visual chrome.

Don't pick this when

The report is a corporate announcement — pick anthropic-paper. The report is a Claude product runbook — pick anthropic-clay. The report is a 20+ minute engineering ramp-up with sticky-section pacing — editorial-parchment is right. The report needs energy or warmth as part of its message — Research will read as understated to a fault.

Voice rules

  • One serif face does all display and body work. Headings get heavier weight and tighter tracking; that is the only visual escalation from body to display.
  • Italic is the standard emphasis style. <em> is italic, not colored.
  • Eyebrows and caption labels use small-caps where possible (font-variant: small-caps) — a paper-typesetting convention.
  • Pull quotes are centered, italic, with typographic quote glyphs ("…") inserted by CSS. They feel like epigraphs, not callouts.
  • Inline code is unboxed — no background fill, only a dotted bottom border (1px dotted var(--rule-strong)). This is the most preprint-feeling treatment.

Explicitly absent from this variation

The corporate-brand palette also includes --blue (#6a9bcc) and --green (#788c5d). Neither appears here — not in callouts, diagrams, links, or pills. Their absence is the language's strongest signal: this is the variation Anthropic would use when only one color should carry meaning, and the rest of the page should read as type on paper.

Diagram-kit aliases

--accent:         var(--orange);
--accent-soft:    var(--orange-soft);
--secondary:      var(--ink-soft);    /* grey — not a color */
--secondary-soft: var(--bg-soft);
Warning. The temptation to introduce a "subtle blue" for links or pull quotes will arise. Resist it. The discipline of a single accent is the entire point — adding even one more hue collapses the language into Paper.
05

Anti-patterns

  • No second accent color. No blue, green, amber, purple, anywhere.
  • No sans-serif body or display. Source Serif Pro carries everything.
  • No colored callout backgrounds. Callouts are a thin left rule on transparent — no fill.
  • No card chrome with borders + shadow + background. Cards are top-and-bottom rules only.
  • No filled inline-code chips. Inline code is a dotted bottom border only.
  • No emoji. No icons.
  • No wide layouts. The 760px main column with 64px right padding is load-bearing.
  • No shadows of any kind. Shadows belong to UI; this is paper.
  • No hover backgrounds on TOC links — color change only.
  • No upright italic-em (a Clay treatment). Italic stays italic.

07

Diagram philosophy

Diagrams in Research are quiet figures, not infographics. Every box is a 1px outline on a transparent ground; labels sit outside the boxes and are connected to them by thin leader-lines where the relationship is not obvious. Text inside the figure uses Source Serif Pro — there is no separate diagram typeface — and the orange ink appears only on the hot role and on the alert region. The register is preprint, not product page.

Role-to-palette map

RoleFillStrokeNotes
defaulttransparentvar(--rule-strong)1px outline, rx="0". No fill — the box is a frame around a label.
hottransparentvar(--orange)The single orange figure on the page; same 1px weight, just the accent hue.
storetransparentvar(--ink-soft)1px; rendered as a doubled rectangle (two concentric outlines, 3px apart) to read as "store" without color.
externaltransparentvar(--ink-soft), dashedstroke-dasharray: 3 3. No second hue — dash signals "outside the system."
warntransparentvar(--orange-deep)Same family as hot; reserved for the alert region.
futuretransparentvar(--ink-ghost), dashedstroke-dasharray: 4 3, low-contrast ghost line.

Alert region pattern

The alert region is the only place orange ink appears outside the hot role. It is rendered as a thin 1px dashed frame in --orange-deep — no fill, no surface tint. Inside the frame, a small-caps caption (alert region — failure path) anchors the region and a nested rectangle pill carries the dominant message. The pattern is visible in Figure 2 between steps 03 and 04.

LockedFrame: 1px --orange-deep dashed, no fill. Pill: 0.75px --orange-deep solid outline, no fill. Alert text in Source Serif small-caps, not a separate display face.
FreeFrame dimensions, pill copy, presence/absence of the inner pill (small alert regions may omit it).

Caption convention

Captions follow the journal preprint convention: an italic Figure N. prefix in Source Serif italic, followed by a sentence-case description that names each labelled region by its (a)(b)(c) sub-letter. The caption sits centered below the figure, separated from it by white space rather than a rule.
